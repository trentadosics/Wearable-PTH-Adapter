Testing wearable electronic circuits at an early stage can be difficult for several reasons:

* Using crocodile clips often results in a messy setup and unreliable connections.
* Conductive thread can also create poor or inconsistent connections when components are sewn together.

As a result, when a circuit does not work, it can be difficult to identify the source of the problem. The issue may come from the circuit design, the components, the connections, or a combination of these factors.

In addition, sewing components into a textile is not convenient during the prototyping phase. Early designs often require modifications, meaning components may need to be removed and re-sewn several times, making the development process slow and cumbersome.
